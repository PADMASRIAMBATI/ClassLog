# Lecture Plan: Simple and Compound Interest

**Course:** Mathematics/Finance

**Chapter:** Interest

**Topics:** Simple Interest & Compound Interest

**1. Topics & Subtopics:**

* **I. Simple Interest:**
    * A. Definition and Formula: Principal, Rate, Time, Interest.
    * B. Calculating Simple Interest: Worked examples with varying time periods (years, months, days).
    * C. Finding Principal, Rate, or Time: Solving for unknowns in the simple interest formula.
    * D. Applications of Simple Interest:  Loans, savings accounts (basic scenarios).
    * E.  Simple Interest vs. Other Interest Types (brief introduction for comparison).

* **II. Compound Interest:**
    * A. Definition and Concept:  Understanding compounding periods (annually, semi-annually, quarterly, monthly).
    * B. Compound Interest Formula:  Derivation and explanation of each variable.
    * C. Calculating Compound Interest: Worked examples with different compounding periods.
    * D. Future Value (FV) Calculations: Determining the final amount after compounding.
    * E. Present Value (PV) Calculations: Determining the initial principal required to achieve a specific future value.
    * F.  Applications of Compound Interest: Investments, loans with compounding interest (more complex scenarios).
    * G. Comparing Simple and Compound Interest:  Illustrating the significant differences over time.


**2. Materials Needed:**

* Whiteboard or projector
* Markers or pens
* Handout with formulas and examples (printable PDF)
* Calculator (scientific calculator recommended)
* PowerPoint presentation (optional, but recommended for visual aids)
* Spreadsheet software (e.g., Excel or Google Sheets) - for demonstrating calculations and visualizing growth.


**3. Learning Objectives:**

* Students will be able to define and calculate simple interest.
* Students will be able to solve for unknown variables in the simple interest formula.
* Students will be able to define and calculate compound interest with different compounding periods.
* Students will be able to calculate future value and present value using the compound interest formula.
* Students will be able to compare and contrast simple and compound interest.
* Students will be able to apply their knowledge to solve real-world problems involving interest calculations.


**4. Timeline Breakdown (50-minute lecture):**

* **5-10 minutes:** Introduction: Review of basic financial concepts (principal, rate, time).  Engage students with a relatable example (e.g., saving for a future purchase).
* **15-20 minutes:** Simple Interest: Definition, formula, worked examples, solving for unknowns.  Include visual aids or real-world examples.
* **10-15 minutes:** Compound Interest: Definition, formula, worked examples with different compounding periods.  Highlight the difference compared to simple interest.
* **5-10 minutes:**  Applications and Comparison:  Real-world scenarios, comparison of simple vs. compound interest (using a graph or table to illustrate the difference over time).  Q&A.


**5. Exercises & Homework:**

* **In-class Activity:**  Solve 2-3 simple interest problems and 2-3 compound interest problems on the whiteboard or in groups.
* **Homework:**  A worksheet with a mix of simple and compound interest problems, including problems that require solving for unknown variables (principal, rate, time, future value, present value).  Include at least one word problem applying the concepts to a real-world situation.


**6. Additional Resources:**

* **Khan Academy:**  Search for "simple interest" and "compound interest" for video tutorials and practice exercises.
* **Investopedia:**  Provides definitions and explanations of financial concepts, including simple and compound interest.
* **Online calculators:**  Numerous websites offer simple and compound interest calculators that can be used to check answers and explore different scenarios.  (Note: Encourage students to understand the underlying calculations rather than solely relying on calculators).
* **Textbooks:**  Refer to relevant chapters in the assigned textbook for supplementary material and additional practice problems.
