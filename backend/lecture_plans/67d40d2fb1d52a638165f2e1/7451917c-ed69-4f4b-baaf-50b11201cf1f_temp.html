Lecture Plan: Simple & Compound Interest

Course: Mathematics/Finance

Chapter: Interest

Lecture Title: Understanding Simple and Compound Interest

1. Topics & Subtopics:

  • I. Simple Interest (60 minutes)
    • A. Definition of Simple Interest
    • B. Formula for Simple Interest: I = P * r * t (Explanation of each variable: Principal (P), Rate (r), Time (t))
    • C. Calculating Simple Interest: Worked examples with varying time periods (years, months, days).
    • D. Finding Principal, Rate, or Time given the other variables. (Solving for P, r, or t)
    • E. Applications of Simple Interest: Loans, investments with simple interest calculations.
  • II. Compound Interest (75 minutes)
    • A. Definition of Compound Interest and the concept of compounding.
    • B. Formula for Compound Interest: A = P (1 + r/n)^(nt) (Explanation of each variable: Principal (P), Rate (r), Time (t), Number of times interest is compounded per year (n)).
    • C. Calculating Compound Interest: Worked examples with different compounding periods (annually, semi-annually, quarterly, monthly).
    • D. Comparing Simple and Compound Interest: Illustrating the differences through examples and graphs.
    • E. Future Value and Present Value: Introducing the concepts and showing calculations.
    • F. Applications of Compound Interest: Savings accounts, investments, mortgages (brief overview).

2. Materials Needed:

  • Whiteboard or Projector
  • Markers or Pens
  • Handout with formulas and key definitions (provided)
  • Calculator (students should bring their own)
  • Slides (PowerPoint or similar) with examples and visuals (provided) – including graphs comparing simple and compound interest.
  • Optional: Spreadsheet software (Excel or Google Sheets) for demonstrating calculations and visualizing growth.

3. Learning Objectives:

Upon completion of this lecture, students will be able to:

  • Define simple and compound interest.
  • Calculate simple interest using the appropriate formula.
  • Calculate compound interest using the appropriate formula for various compounding periods.
  • Compare and contrast simple and compound interest.
  • Apply these concepts to solve real-world problems related to loans and investments.
  • Understand the concepts of future value and present value (in the context of compound interest).

4. Timeline Breakdown:

  • (0-15 mins): Introduction – Review of basic percentage calculations. Introduce the concept of interest and its importance.
  • (15-45 mins): Simple Interest – Cover subtopics A-E. Include several worked examples and allow time for student questions.
  • (45-50 mins): Short break/Q&A addressing any lingering questions on simple interest.
  • (50-75 mins): Compound Interest – Cover subtopics A-D. Use visuals to emphasize the difference between simple and compound interest growth.
  • (75-90 mins): Compound Interest (continued) – Cover subtopics E & F. Introduce Future and Present Value concepts briefly.
  • (90-105 mins): Q&A and wrap-up.

5. Exercises & Homework:

  • In-class Activity (30 mins): Students work in pairs to solve a series of simple and compound interest problems.
  • Homework:

    • A comparison question: Calculate and compare the total interest earned on $1000 invested for 5 years at 5% interest, compounded annually vs. simple interest.
    • (Optional) Research and present a real-world example of simple or compound interest.
